SEA 26 Drone Shows

Enjoy Seattle’s one-of-a-kind celebrations after each World CupTM soccer match

 

Where: International Fountain at Seattle Center

When: Evenings on Seattle Match Days

 

Visit Seattle is excited to showcase first-of-its-kind match-night drone shows to celebrate our city’s FIFA World CupTM soccer matches!

At nighttime after each match Seattle hosts, a constellation of drones will light up the sky and broadcast the match’s final score and each team’s flag alongside the iconic Space Needle above Seattle Center.

The drone shows will be free to the public.

Show Times and Location

Drone Show Times

The dates and times for the drone shows are as follows:

  • Monday, June 15 show at 10pm (Belgium vs. Egypt)
  • Friday, June 19 showtime TBD (USA vs. Australia)
  • Wednesday, June 24 show at 10pm (Boznia-Herzegovina vs. Qatar)
  • Friday, June 26 show after 11pm (Egypt vs. IR Iran)
  • Wednesday, July 1 show at 11:30pm (Match 82)
  • Monday, July 6 showtime TBD (Match 94)

 

Viewing Locations

Drones will light up the sky alongside the iconic Space Needle above Seattle Center.

For ideal viewing of the performances, we recommend viewing from Fisher Pavilion or the area around the International Fountain at Seattle Center.

How to get to Seattle Center

Whether you’re cheering in the stadium, gathering in parks, or exploring neighborhoods, you’re a part of a celebration that stretches from the urban core to the great outdoors.

Did you know?

A thriving and distinctive waterfront city, Seattle is no stranger to hosting world-class sporting events. From the 2023 MLB All-Star Game and 2024 NHL Winter Classic at T-Mobile Park to FIFA World Cup 2026 matches at Lumen Field, Seattle is ready to bring any sporting event to life. Plus, as the home of pickleball, our city’s enthusiasm for athletic effort and teamwork is contagious, no matter who’s playing.

Seattle is Built on Native Land

The city of Seattle resides on the traditional land of the Coast Salish Peoples, past and present. We honor with gratitude our shared land and waterways, as well as the history and heritage of our indigenous neighbors.
