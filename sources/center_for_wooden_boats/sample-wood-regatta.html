Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D. Regatta: September 19th, 2026

Registration coming soon for The 2026 Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D. Regatta!

Join us for a day of racing in our Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D. Regatta. We will be accepting registrations for racers 12 years old and up. The regatta will be a day long event including a BBQ after the racing (tickets sold separately).

About the Race:

The Center for Wooden Boats’ annual Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D. (Wooden Open & One Design) Regatta honors the best-known name in wooden boatbuilding and yachting in the Pacific Northwest. Norman C. Blanchard’s Blanchard Boat Company turned out more than 2,000 boats in 60+ years during the past century. Quality, performance and longevity are the hallmarks of Norm Blanchard.

We want to extend the race invitation to CWB members as well as local racers who may or may not have participated in our past regattas. There will be several races (depending on wind) over the course of the day. This is a non-spinnaker event. Classes will be “boats 16 feet and under” and “boats over 16 feet”. Each class will have its own start. There will be a break at midday for lunch then back out for the final races of the day.

You can bring your own boat. If you choose this option, be sure to let us know your boat rating (if possible) when you finalize your registration. The registration fee covers you and your crew.

CWB boats will also be available as long as at least one blue-card holder is aboard. We can assign you to a boat or you and your friends can form a crew and race together on a boat. In either case each participant will need to register separately. Boat assignment is first come first served.

Separate prizes will be awarded for each class.  For this regatta, we will have a small boat class (16ft or less) and a Large boat class (Greater than 16 feet). If you are bringing your own boat, but do not know the rating/handicap number of your vessel, you are still invited to race. Unrated vessels will not be competing for ranking in the races. 

Who Can Participate?

  • Racers

    • Youth: Ages 12 to 18. (Parents must complete the Youth waiver form. Sailors must have completed Youth Sailing 2 or equivalent)

    • Adult: Ages 18 and up. (Must have a Livery Card if planning to use CWB boats) schedule your check out HERE!

  • CWB Boats

    • You can register to participate on CWB a boat! Boats will be assigned on the day of the race on a first-come first-assigned basis.

      CWB's LARGE (>2 crew) Sailboat Regatta Fleet Includes:

    • 1 Thunderbird Kittiwake - sloop rig

    • 3 Blanchard Junior Knockabouts - sloop rig

    • CWB's SMALL (1-2 crew) Sailboat Regatta Fleet Includes:

    • 10 El Toros - Bermuda catboats

    • 1 Pelican - lug rig

    • 1 Catboat Riff - gaff rig

    • 1 Port Madison Pram Mary I - sloop rig

    • 1 Ed Monk Sloop L’Hirondelle - sloop rig

  • Bring your own Boat and Crew

    • Open to all wooden boats - from El Toros to large racer cruisers

    • Non-wood versions of traditional wood one-designs (e.g. Lightnings, Thunderbirds, El Toros). email us at adultsailing@cwb.org if you have questions

Tentative Race Day Schedule: Saturday, September 19, 2026

      • 9:30 AM – 10:30:  Registration

      • 10:30 AM – 11:00 Skippers meeting (Boathouse dock)

      • 11:00 – 12:00 Rigging and Launching

      • 12:00 – First Race

      • Break after second race

      • 3:00 PM - Last Race Start

      • 4:30 - 6:00 - BBQ and Awards!

Stay up to date on registration and race information by checking our social media and this page! You can also get information by subscribing to our monthly newsletter. If you have immediate questions, please reach out to adultsailing@cwb.org

2025 Race Results

It felt just like old times at CWB, except the weather was better! Thanks to all the volunteers and staff! Special thanks to the Livery staff who completed assembly and rigging of two Blanchard knockouts! One of the boats, Pamela, still did not have a rig at 6 PM on Friday! Only a terrific last minute effort by a dedicated team of volunteers and staff led by Kathleen Lothrop managed to get the boat together and ready to race.

What a day it was! Unbelievably sunny skies and winds building almost to 10 kts kept the big boats moving and the little boats excited. Six boats over 16 feet and twelve smaller boats raced. We had visiting El Toro’s, a Dragon, a Blanchard Senior knockabout, a sprit boat, a Mirror, and a Thunderbird. Four races were held with a short lunch break in between. Anyone who raced all four had the option to throw out one of the races.
First place overall was taken by Ken Rasmussen on his El Toro, 11817. Ken will get his name on the Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D. Regatta, perpetual trophy.

Results for each class:
Large boats:
1st: Dragon
2nd: Snow Goose tied with Pamela
3rd: Footloose

Small boats:
First: El Toro 11817
2nd: Mirror “HOPE”
3rd: El Toro ”Swallow “

Thanks to all the wonderful participants and the volunteers and staff who made it happen.

2024 Regatta Highlights

We got through the 19th annual Norm Blanchard W.O.O.D Regatta with high clouds and NO rain! Eight boats registered from outside of CWB.  The remainder were CWB boats.  Boats ranged in size from 8’ to 29’.   

We completed a total of 4 races over the course of the day.  There was good wind for the start of the first race.  However, the southerly wind slowly died away so that the second race was shortened to allow time for lunch.  After the break, a breeze had come up from the north to provide good sailing for the last two races of the day. 

Special thanks to Lavengro for being our committee boat, Gary Bangs our fearless race committee leader, and all the volunteers that contributed time to make the regatta possible! Next year we will be looking to hold 3 regattas at CWB.  The Tiny Boat Regatta and W.O.O.D. Regatta will have their usual places in June and September respectively.  We would also like to add one in late March so please stay tuned!

3rd Place Allan Johnson on Sommerfugl (Dragon)

2022 Regatta Highlights

 

Video produced by CWB volunteer J.A. Wiley

 

The 2022 Regatta was one great, wonderful adventure with good breeze and bright skies.

We were joined by over 30 boats and 150+ community members for a full day of racing. All 13 boats from our own livery participated plus 5 Norm Blanchard-built wooden boats (each 70+ years old!) sailed in this homage to the formative boatbuilder and designer. The fleet was diverse, ranging from 8 to 32 feet across Pelicans, Thunderbirds, Lightnings, and El Toros, with sailors ages 10 to 70+, some bringing decades of experience and some brand new SailNOW! grads.


Thank you to our community for coming out, helping out, and enjoying this beautiful lake we call home!
